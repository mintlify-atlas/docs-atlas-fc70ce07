Threatest lets you validate that your threat detection rules actually fire when they should. Define a scenario, detonate an attack technique, and confirm the expected security signal was generated — automatically, in CI/CD or locally.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define a scenario">
    Describe an attack technique to detonate and the security alert you expect to see — as YAML or Go code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Detonate the attack">
    Threatest executes the technique using your chosen detonator: local commands, SSH, Stratus Red Team, or AWS CLI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the alert">
    Threatest polls your security platform (Datadog) until it finds a matching alert correlated to the exact detonation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report results">
    Pass/fail results are reported to stdout and optionally written to a JSON file for CI/CD integration.
  </Step>
</Steps>
